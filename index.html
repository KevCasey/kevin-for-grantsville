Budget Accountability

Population vs. Spending: Grantsville’s population has grown by approximately 11% over the past three years. However, city budgets have grown by over 20% in many departments—including more than 25% increases in areas like engineering services and public works.
What's Missing: Many spending resolutions—such as police wage increases or beautification contracts—were passed without any dollar amounts disclosed to the public.

Key Issues

City Debt

Did you know? As of the end of fiscal year 2024, Grantsville City holds $19,073,428 in total outstanding debt.
  • Revenue Bonds: $12,768,512 — secured by city revenue sources
  • Remaining Debt: $6.3 million — includes building leases and equipment financing
FY2026 Debt Service Costs:
  • Justice Center: $73,000 principal + $55,900 interest
  • Fire Department: $72,000 principal + $36,680 interest
  • Library: $98,000 principal + $37,250 interest

These payments total over $372,000 annually — and that’s before factoring in new projects. If new infrastructure is financed with bonds, it could increase this obligation significantly.

As your Councilmember, I will ensure all new debt proposals are publicly posted with full cost, interest, and long-term impacts disclosed in plain language.

Transparency

Did you know? Most of the resolutions passed by the City Council don’t list the actual cost of the proposals.
  • Citizens are expected to trust what they can’t see. I will push for full fiscal transparency on every resolution.
  • Meeting minutes rarely reference the financial impact of approved actions.
  • Budget categories are often broad, with little detail available to taxpayers.

I will push for itemized cost estimates, publicly accessible procurement breakdowns, and digital dashboards so every citizen knows where their money goes.
